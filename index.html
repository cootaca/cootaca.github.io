Warren Buffett

The Genius Behind Berkshire Hathaway

General Biography

Warren Buffett was born on August 30, 1930, in Omaha, Nebraska. He was born to his mother, Leila, and his father, Howard, a former stockbroker turned senator. Buffett showed an incredible acumen for business and money from an early age. He was able to resell Coca-Cola bottles when he was only six years old, and by the time Buffett was eleven, he was already investing in the stock market. Though Buffett showed incredible interest in finance, he was always skeptical about attending higher educational institutions. However, his father convinced him to study up to a graduate level. Buffett ended up pursuing the Masters in Economics program at Columbia University, where the legendary Ben Graham mentored him.

Professional Background

After graduating from Columbia University, Warren Buffett returned home to take a job at his father's brokerage. Soon after, he met Susie Thompson, who soon became his first wife. During that time, Buffett had investments in a Omaha gas station and some real estate. That's when Ben Graham called Buffett to offer him an opportunity to work for his brokerage, and Buffett promptly accepted the offer. However, Graham and Buffett had very different philosophies about investing. Graham was intensely focused on just the numbers of the corporation, while Buffett was more interested in the company's management. In the six years he worked at Graham's Brokerage, Buffett grew his capital from 9,800 dollars to 140,000 dollars (equivalent to 1,333,000 today when adjusted for inflation). Buffett brought his stash back to Omaha and decided to create Buffett Associates, Ltd. Buffett managed to convince close relatives and other associates to give him their money to grow, which is the service he offered. By the end of the year, he had over 300,000 dollars in capital. Buffett only took 25% percent of profits over 4 percent, a low fee, and produced incredibly high returns, making the firm attractive for prospective investors.

Over the next five years, Buffett generated an impressive 251% profit (the Dow was up only 74% during that time). Buffett Associates evolved into Buffett Partnership Ltd., and ten years after its founding, its assets were up 1150%. During that period, Buffett established a 49 percent stake in Berkshire Hathaway. At that point, Buffett decided to buy the company outright for 8.6 million dollars. Buffett's addition of an insuring and banking division in the company proved to be far more lucrative than the textile industry, Berkshire Hathaway's former focus. Textile profits were a minor 45,000 dollars that paled compared to the banking and insurance profits of 4.7 million dollars. As the banking division continued to grow, Buffett decided to buy See's candies, a chocolate company that Californians are willing to pay a steep premium for. Buffett also began to build equity in GEICO and managed to turn it around from steep losses to enormous profits. Eventually, GEICO became a full subsidiary of Berkshire Hathaway. Berkshire Hathaway continued to make incredibly profitable acquisitions consistently. Buffett purchased Scott and Fetzer and the Nebraska Furniture Market. Buffett also bought 7 percent of Coca Cola for about 1 billion dollars (Berkshire's stake in Coca Cola is currently worth 22 billion dollars). Berkshire Hathaway continued to show astronomical growth and slowly grew to the conglomerate we know today.

Agent Of Change

Warren Buffett revolutionized the investing and business world. With a focus on management and the product ideal rather than solely numbers or unfounded speculation (as was done in Wall Street's old days), Buffett was perhaps the first significant value investor. In addition, Buffett was able to successfully take over businesses that were struggling due to poor management rather than the product itself and generate massive growth within those companies. Buffett spurred a new age of investing and even a new model of business management.

Pivotal Issues

Warren Buffett wasn't able to build one of the world's largest holding companies without encountering massive issues. Right as Buffett started his career, he made a mistake buying an Omaha gas station positioned directly across the street from a Texaco station. Of course, the vast majority of traffic in that area refilled their gas from the Texaco station because it had name recognition. Buffett used this experience to put a heavier emphasis on brand perception and recognition moving forward. One of the major controversies that Berkshire Hathaway faced was the Saloman Brothers incident. By 1990 Berkshire Hathaway had acquired a 12% interest in the Salomon Brothers investment bank. In 1990 news came out that a rogue trader from Salomon Brothers had submitted bids in excess of Treasury rules, and the CEO of the bank failed to reprimand the trader. The treasury department was threatening to destroy the company when Buffett stepped in and negotiated a 290 million dollar fine to be levied against the investment bank instead of obliterating the bank outright. Another challenge Berkshire Hathaway faced was the market crash of 1987 in which Berkshire Hathaway lost 1/4 of its market cap; however, Buffett managed to push through that tumultuous period with a clear head and grew Berkshire Hathaway to become far more profitable and valuable.

Philanthropy

Warren Buffett has signed the giving pledge meaning that he will give away more than 99% of his wealth. Warren Buffett has already given tens of billions of dollars to charity. Buffett donates to a vast range of charities such as the Bill and Melinda Gates foundation and numerous charities involved with humanitarian aid.

My Perspective

I like the way Buffett presents himself, and the actions he takes. Buffett takes total responsibility as the chief executive officer of a massive corporation. In addition, he acknowledges his own luck in making it to where he is today. He also pledged to donate the overwhelming majority of wealth for philanthropic causes, so he must feel some ethical/moral responsibility to do so. So overall, I gained a positive perception of Buffett as I researched him.
